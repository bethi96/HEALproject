Step 1: EDUCATE rural East Texas communities' health outcomes


East Texas suffers from a lack of access to health care, correlated to lower life expectancy at birth. In Texas, life expectancy at birth is 78.3 years.However, this varies significantly in East Texas, with 72.4 years being the lowest. Northeast Texas experiences higher mortality rates for each of the five leading causes of death in the U.S. (Heart Disease, Cancer, Chronic Lower Respiratory Diseases, Stroke, and unintentional injury.


Step 2: INFORM communities about the National Library of Medicine (NLM)


The National Library of Medicine (NLM), on the National Institutes of Health campus in Bethesda, Maryland, has been a center of information innovation since its founding in 1836. What is Health Literacy?

Health Literacy is the degree to which individuals have the capacity to obtain, process, and understand basic health information needed to mke appropriate health decisions.

Why is Health Literacy important?


People with low health literacy skills are more likely to:


  • Have poor health outcomes, including hospital stays and emergency room visits.
  • Make medication errors.
  • Have trouble managing chronic diseases
  • Skip preventive services, like flu shots.
